What the Best Founders Are Building

Let me share three patterns I'm seeing from the founders who are getting this right:

The "copilot for the maestro" approach. The best startups aren't trying to replace the experienced factory manager or the master baker. They're building tools that augment their expertise. One founder told me: "Our AI doesn't replace the maestro â€” it gives him superpowers." That's exactly the right framing.

Vertical-specific, horizontal tech. Smart founders are taking horizontal AI capabilities (computer vision, predictive analytics, NLP) and packaging them for specific verticals with deep domain knowledge. A generic "AI quality control" platform doesn't work. An AI system that understands the 47 defect types specific to Hass avocados? That's a business.

Hardware-light, insight-heavy. The winning model isn't selling expensive hardware. It's using commodity cameras and sensors paired with sophisticated software. Low capex for the customer, high margins for the startup, recurring revenue from the insights layer.
